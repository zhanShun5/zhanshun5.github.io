ABOUT ME

I'M 24 years old My first consol was a rip off version of a gameboy.
With seven I started to play Warcraft 3 on my cousins laptop. Since then almost every year, I replay the main campain.

Motivation

Having quit my previousely soul wretching studies made me realize that I want to make all sorts of art instead.

And for me games are the ideal medium to achive this desire, since they encapsulate a vaste varity of art disciplies, such as Arts, Writing, and Music (to name the most forward ones).

Since then I've started to have a 9to5 (somethimes 12to5) practicing game development and everything around it. [And I admit, It wasn't uncommon for me at the begining to even dream about Godot's script manager or placing tiles on the girdmap.]
Through my previouse study and various coding projects I've learned to maintain clean code and have a wide understanding of coding. As well as being comfortable with learning new programing languages and applications.
Music is most of the times my form of meditation. When making a track I tend to go with the Einstellung : "As long as it sounds good and fits.". Understanding that Music Theory is merely a suggestion. ==\[Leave out last sentence?==] I tried myself on differnt genres, however, I feel the most comfortable with tracks that emit an ambient vibe to them. Attempting to resemble the soundtrack of 'Disco Elysium' and 'Who's Lila'.
Although, creating Visuals, such as your own sprites and larger and more complex 3D models, are still not an ability I'm too comfortable with,
yet are on the top of my priority list.

Virtual Garden

- Pooled Upgrades - similar to TFT's pool but not quite
- Incremental game with gird interactions - Incremental games to me are about a feeling of progress. From making little to exordinarly absurd amounts of currency. - But progression is not just clicking one button over and over, i needs to feel desevered and earned, there needs to be time spent improvign. - So the one thing I needed to do was introduce friction to progression. - Limiting the gird space/increasing the platnst needed, to create startegies. - either lots of plants or lots of tools? What do I remove to make space for the new objects? - The upgrades and plants are contesting with each other, it's up to the player to find their own most optical strategy. - In the end both are needed - Increasing the add-plant button by a bit each press, so that even later into the game, it would'nt be too easy to get new plants - the same with the add-xp buttons. - Yet since this is the first version, and is intented as an introduction, it feelt right to implement an aid to gain currency, - This is why peroridcally coins fall from the screen. (- Balancing testing needed to be done to get a decent feel.)
- Plants and tool were drawn by my Sister, the rest from an asset pack by Seth. - Plants were drawn in a differnt artstyle, since the plants in the asset pack meshed too much to the background, afterall, they are meant to be decorative. - Now they standout more and seem more like an interactable object.
- an huge help was the GodotGameLab Autobattler Tutorial
- Further development plans are to create more plants, but also an additional resources, there furthermore, increasing the startegic possibilitieso of which resource to focuse on. - I also considered adding an addition play area. The player would switch from one play area to the other. In this playarea there would grow other stuff with different rules. - The idea of crows and a scarecrow are some that I want to develop, they would create a more interactivity to the game.
- Playable on the browswer - has saving features (only when games downloaded)
