Brief Journey to the FarLands

Started off as a 2D Platformer that uses interations with light and darkenss. (See pictures 123) The 2D Platformer was supposed to be an open world expiereince akin to that of "Hollow Knight" and the exploration of "Noita". However the movement didn't feel correct. Restricting. Lacking another dimension. Another dimension means more problems to consider but also greater freedoms in how to desgine mechanics and the world. I under-estimated an 3D Open world platformer; An Idea that I didn't abandon, but postponed for now. Instead I choose for a linear platforming, one way forward, to some degrees, similar to that of "Sonic Adventure", because of the dash mechanic I implemented and the increased acceleration one has from dashing off slopes and alike.

Over all I like the 3D Platformer. The movement feel faster, wider, and a great sense of speed is feelt crossing great distnace. Thats why the name. Short Game, far lands (to cross).
